COPPER/SOD1 RESEARCH PROJECT IMPLEMENTATION PLAN
==============================================

Based on the proven data-gathering methodology from Histidine and Alpha-Synuclein projects, this plan outlines the systematic approach for researching copper homeostasis and SOD1 pathways in bacterial systems.

PROJECT OVERVIEW
================

BIOLOGICAL FOCUS: Copper Homeostasis and Superoxide Dismutase Systems
- Primary target: Bacterial homologs of copper-dependent antioxidant systems
- Secondary focus: Copper transport, storage, and regulatory mechanisms
- Clinical relevance: Parkinson's disease SOD1 pathology and copper deficiency

RESEARCH OBJECTIVES:
1. Identify bacterial copper homeostasis subsystems
2. Map SOD1/superoxide dismutase distribution across genomes
3. Analyze copper transporter and chaperone systems
4. Study correlations with oxidative stress response pathways
5. Build predictive models for copper-dependent phenotypes

PHASE 1: TARGET IDENTIFICATION & SEARCH STRATEGY
==============================================

1. PRIMARY BIOLOGICAL TARGETS
-----------------------------

Core Copper/SOD1 Pathway Components:
a) Superoxide Dismutases:
   - SOD1 (Cu/Zn superoxide dismutase) - primary target
   - SOD2 (Mn superoxide dismutase) - comparative analysis
   - SOD3 (extracellular Cu/Zn SOD) - if present in bacteria

b) Copper Transport Systems:
   - CTR1/SLC31A1 homologs (copper transporters)
   - ATP7A/ATP7B homologs (copper-transporting ATPases)
   - CopA, CopB copper efflux systems

c) Copper Chaperones:
   - CCS (copper chaperone for SOD1)
   - Cox17 (cytochrome c oxidase copper chaperone)
   - Atox1 (antioxidant protein 1)

d) Copper Storage/Regulation:
   - Metallothioneins (MT1, MT2)
   - CueR/CopY copper-responsive regulators
   - CusR/CusS copper sensing systems

e) Related Antioxidant Systems:
   - Catalase (CAT)
   - Glutathione peroxidase (GPX)
   - Thioredoxin systems

2. BACTERIAL COPPER SYSTEM RESEARCH
----------------------------------

Known Bacterial Copper Systems:
- Cop operon (copper resistance)
- Cue system (copper efflux)
- Pco system (plasmid copper resistance)
- Sco proteins (copper chaperones)
- Multicopper oxidases
- Blue copper proteins

Target Organisms:
- E. coli (well-characterized copper systems)
- Bacillus species (diverse antioxidant systems)
- Pseudomonas (environmental copper exposure)
- Mycobacterium (oxidative stress resistance)
- Marine bacteria (metal-rich environments)
